One of ways in which I try to keep up to date with what's going on in the industry (and more specifically in development) is by listening to podcasts. It's a perfect way to keep me busy and engaged during my commute to work (unless I'm going by train, in which case I always prefer to read a book). The podcasts I found usually most interesting are:

- [Stack Overflow](https://blog.stackoverflow.com/category/podcasts/) with [Jeff](https://www.codinghorror.com/blog/) and [Joel](https://www.joelonsoftware.com/)
- [Hansel minutes](https://www.hanselminutes.com/)
- [.NET Rocks!](https://www.dotnetrocks.com/)
- [Herding Code](https://herdingcode.com/)

Rather than browsing individual sites I have created a  little [podcast pipe](https://pipes.yahoo.com/mfloryan/podcasts ".NET podcasts pipe") ([RSS feed](https://pipes.yahoo.com/pipes/pipe.run?_id=285d0838c273d957fc560e19862b1ba5&_render=rss "RSS feed of .NET podcast pipe")) using  Yahoo! pipes which aggregates all of the above and gives me a convenient feed for my Google Reader.
