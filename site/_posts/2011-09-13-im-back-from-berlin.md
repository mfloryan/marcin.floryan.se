[![](/assets/images/2011/09/6135333627_1a049c6e4c_m.jpg "Olaf opening ALE2011")](https://www.flickr.com/photos/mfloryan/6135333627/in/set-72157627519257013/)I'm back from Berlin.

I'm back from the amazing [#ALE2011 unconference](https://ale2011.eu/).

A week ago we still didn't know if it will really work. The five months of preparations were a great journey for the organisers. Now was the time to see what we would all experience as participants.

I can tell you now. It worked; It worked well. In fact, it rocked! It was simply amazing.

Feedback was coming thick and fast from all directions and it was good. People from all around Europe were finding in Berlin what they often couldn't find in their local communities. They came to grow their network; our network.

Being part of the organising team I was particularly impressed with the comments along the lines of: _"Wow. It's the first time I see [...] at a conference. Every conference should do that"_. I'm sure we didn't invent all of these things, some we did, and some perhaps were done for the first time at such scale. The main things that got mentioned were:

- Our special [children and spouses program](https://systemagility.com/2011/09/11/happy-memories-of-the-ale2011-spouse-and-kids-program/) so people could make the trip to Berlin a family trip
- Twitter names printed on badges, as many participants knew each other only from twitter
- Dinner with a stranger - an opportunity to meet new people in small groups
- Explicit focus on diversity - bringing people from all across Europe together
- [Sofas instead of chairs](https://ale2011.eu/why-sofas-unconference)
- [CybeDojo](https://jonjagger.blogspot.com/p/cyberdojo.html) as an ice-breaker (by [Jon Jagger](https://www.jaggersoft.com/)) at the start of the conference to get people \*coding\* together
- The flexible nature of the programme changed on the spot based on your feedback

I hope other conference organisers are looking and will decide to pick some of the ideas turning this [crazy event into a leader](https://www.hanoulle.be/2010/05/how-to-create-a-movement/).

![](/assets/images/2011/09/IMG_6503.jpg "ALE in the Bar")For me, personally, the experience of meeting all the people in Berlin, I was so eagerly waiting for, was amazing. It's not often that I go to meet two hundred people, most of whom I never seen before, and feel that I can immediately start a friendly and enthusiastic discussion. Some of the people I met, for the first time in my life, felt like really close friends! I wasn't expecting that.

[I set myself a small challenge](https://marcin.floryan.pl/blog/2011/09/going-to-berlin "Going to Berlin") to bring back three new inspirations from #ALE2011, I brought many more. Here are my top three:

- **[Feature Injection](https://www.infoq.com/news/2009/05/feature-injection-comics)** - I have read about it before, and knew of the concept but to hear it explained from the horse’s mouth (or rather Father Christmas’s mouth) gave me a new perspective and a much deeper understanding. The idea of looking at analysis as going in the opposite direction to development in the feedback cycle is just genius. You have to see that circle drawn by Chris.
- **[Beyond Budgeting](https://www.bbrt.org/beyond-budgeting/beybud.html)** - this was a completely new idea for me, and Bjarte gave a brilliant presentation introducing to us, what seems like common sense, applied at corporate level and setting example for organisations to follow
- **Unlimited Warranty** - in his lightning talk [Yves](https://www.hanoulle.be/) encouraged us to give to all our customers unlimited warranty so that they can get in touch and get help and answers from us after we've done our assignment - forever. I love the idea. Yes I'll be doing that. I think I'm actually doing that and now I will also be conscious of it and explicit about it.

![](/assets/images/2011/09/CheckpointCharlie.jpg "Checkpoint Charlie")As it happened, our venue was very close to [Checkpoint Charlie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Checkpoint_Charlie). I only realised this when we went for a short walk with [Franck](https://twitter.com/#!/FranckDepierre) to print some missing materials. I thought it was interesting but only realised the full significance of the fact after [Oana](https://twitter.com/#!/ojuncu) [wrote about it](https://oanasagile.blogspot.com/2011/09/ale2011-berlin-fall-of-wall.html). _Damn right!_ We couldn't have chosen a better location for the first ALE conference. I was in Berlin as a child, when the wall was still standing. _I couldn't cross. I lived on the other side._ If it was still there, I wouldn't be allowed to meet our "[Token American](https://theitriskmanager.wordpress.com/2011/09/13/a-l-e-2011-the-relationship-factory/)" let alone follow [some ideas](https://agilemanifesto.org) created ten years ago in USA. It really moved me, we are so lucky that we can freely share our ideas and I'm proud to have been able to take a small part in facilitating it.

I want to join [Olaf](https://twitter.com/#!/olaflewitz) in saying [Thank you for ALE2011](https://hhgttg.de/blog/2011/09/11/thank-you-for-ale2011/) to you all. _Thank you._ I also want to add small personal appreciation for [Jurgen](https://www.jurgenappelo.com/) who, apart from everything else, stood up to the challenge and drew an amazing [red dragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flag_of_Wales) :)

I showed [some pictures](https://www.flickr.com/photos/mfloryan/sets/72157627519257013/) from the three days to my family. From the pictures alone, spiced up a bit perhaps with my enthusiastic commentary, they said: _"Wow. It's amazing. You look like a bunch of crazy nerds who are actually having fun! Creative and engaged."_

Oh yes, _we did have fun_ and I'm so glad I can be part of this merry crowd.
